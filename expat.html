You're Not Alone in This

Moving to Italy is wonderful and overwhelming in equal measure.

The dream is real — the slow mornings, the food, the light, the sense that life is actually being lived. But the process of actually getting there? The residency registration, the healthcare system, the apartment hunt in a language you're still learning, the forms that no one can explain clearly even in Italian — that part is hard.

I moved from the US to Naples and figured most of it out the slow and painful way. Now I help others skip the worst of it. Not a travel agency, not a law firm — just someone on the ground who knows how things actually work and is happy to make a few phone calls on your behalf.

What I Take Off Your Plate

The things that trip everyone up

🏠

Finding an Apartment

Vetting listings, spotting red flags, understanding what's normal vs. what's a scam, and accompanying you (in person or virtually) when it matters.

📋

Residency Registration

Getting your anagrafe appointment, understanding what documents you need, and making sure nothing falls through the cracks at the comune.

🏥

Healthcare & ASL

Enrolling in the national health system, finding an English-speaking doctor, and understanding how the Italian healthcare system actually functions day to day.

🏦

Banking & Finances

Opening an Italian bank account, setting up utilities, understanding how payments work — and avoiding the places that make it unnecessarily painful.

🗺️

Neighborhood Guidance

Matching you to the right part of Naples for who you actually are — not the tourist version of the city, but the real one.

📞

Local Network Access

Trusted contacts built over years — mechanics, accountants, landlords, doctors, fixers. The people who make life here actually work.
